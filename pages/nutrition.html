The basics

“The law of conservation of energy states that the total energy of an isolated system is constant; energy can be transformed from one form to another but can be neither created nor destroyed.”

It is applicable to the entire universe…including you, and +99% of fat people do not have a medical condition or a genetic problem, they simply have a behavior problem related to shoveling too much shit in their mouths.

What to eat…and not to eat

  • Keep "good" carbs like bread, oats, ... to a minimum, and stay away from all refined sugars and mark of the beast stuff like HFCS. Excess of carbs is why we are seeing an obesity and type 2 diabetes epidemic in most industrialized countries
  • Keep clear of religion-like movements - e.g. veganism, paelo, carnivore - peddled by fanatics, scam artists or just out of anecdotal experience
  • No chips, soft drinks - i.e. empty calories with lots of glucose and/or laden with salt
  • No fast food, comfort food
  • No fruit juice (lots of sugar in those)
    No bacon, cake, cupcakes, beer, cappuccino, … it’s part of the folklore but crap
  • Eat fresh (and clean) vegetables (mostly, avoid cooking them is possible) and fruits (some, but again watch out for sugars)
  • Eat simple unprocessed lean animal products likes beef, chicken, eggs, turkey, fish. and stay away from bacon (yes, I know), ham, sausage, terrines, ...
  • Olive oil is your friendly and healthy fat
  • I’m a bit fan of steam cooking and also of my George Foreman grill 😛
  • Water (lots) and you do not need to put something in it, but an occasional beer or wine glass is OK
  • Nuts like brazil, walnut, pecan, pistachio, almond, ... are great, but watch out for the quantity (because of the oil)


Everyday meals for me, adjusted for energy expanded, if travelling or a special occasion:

  • Breakfast: Porridge with fresh blueberries or dried apples with cinnamon with a pomelo and a coffee
  • Lunch: A salad (likely Niçoise) or from a salad bar with only vegetables, some form of simple carb (e.g. rice, lentils), and light cheese perhaps or a yoghurt
  • Diner: a fish, or white meat, fresh greens…if at all, you need to keep it small and simple, otherwise it will disturb your sleep
  • Snacks: if needed for whatever reason, nuts or a fruit


Yes, it sounds very boring, but it’s good for you, predictable and consistent = no nasty surprises
