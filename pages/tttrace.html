Handling of a race event - TTT 3x laps “volcano flats”

Last updated Sun 15-Sep-2019 --- suggestions, recommendations, requests, comments, … or just that you found it useful -> sigma.fusion at pm.me

A race is not just about showing up and hammering through. You have a goal to win, which needs to be prepared. For this kind of race, you are also “flying” blind, you cannot just soft pedal the end if you know you a gap/safety net. Teams are staggered with different start times, so you cannot rely on the clock, you more or less should try to deliver through the best of your abilities and cross your fingers that it is good enough.

1. Previous TTT race post-results analysis


https://www.zwiftpower.com/events.php?zid=217424

A win on a relatively flat terrain, which is advantageous to teams as they can better leverage the flats and take turns, but with only 8 secs of margin over 47 mins = razor thin. Distance at 36 km is long enough that you cannot just hammer this with power, but not that long to qualify as endurance.





Xert data analysis


There are “good” and then lucky wins. While the results were good enough with 360W normalized watts over 47:00 (2820 secs) flat for this one, potential based on calculator (numbers are slightly higher now than last week) was in fact higher and not used – which is no bueno. A better fit would have been to shoot for 380/5.9W or more power on the small rises where the teams have less of a draft advantage.



In fact, analysis shows that the “tank” was far from empty as MPA was almost at full strength. You should aim for MPA <= power actually delivered through decreasing slop at the finish line for a TT, as you don’t intend to sprint the finish.
